Research

I study social barriers to children’s motivation and ways to reduce them so that children can thrive.

How we choose to allocate our effort – when to persist and when to give up – shapes the trajectory of our lives, from the skills we develop to the relationships we form to the person we become. Even young infants make calculated, rational decisions about when to embrace or forgo a challenge1,2.

Importantly, children do not make these decisions in a vacuum: they rely both on adults in their immediate environments and messages in their broader culture to determine what endeavors are worth their time. I am interested in how these (sometimes well-intentioned) social influences go astray and undermine children’s motivation to learn and pursue domains in which they could have otherwise thrived. To answer these questions, I use a variety of methods, including surveys, randomized-controlled experiments, computational modeling, and physiological measurement.

In one line of work, I examine how underrepresentation and social stereotypes lead girls and children of color to question their abilities and belonging in high-status domains (e.g., STEM, leadership)3,4,5,6 and how leveraging children’s imagination and psychological distancing may ameliorate some of these harmful effects7.

In another line of work, I explore parents’ tendency to restrict children’s autonomy by “taking over” and completing developmentally appropriate tasks for them. Specifically, I am interested in better understanding parents’ minds during effortful child action (e.g., parents’ beliefs about children’s learning and capabilities, parents’ emotional state) in order to help create more supportive environments for children to autonomously learn and explore8.

One of my favorite aspects of science is the ability to collaborate and learn from others both within and outside of academia. So whether you are a researcher, student, parent, teacher (or none of the above!), please feel free to reach out to me if you have questions or would like to discuss the topics above.
